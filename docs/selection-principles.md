# Portfolio Selection Principles

This portfolio is intentionally curated around real chemistry and molecular-data problems rather than generic demonstration notebooks.

## What Was Included

- projects that show actual simulation or molecular-data workflow ownership
- workflows that move from setup to interpretable result
- evidence of method breadth across CADD/QSAR, molecular DFT, periodic DFT, and MD simulation
- source code and analysis organization that support reproducible computational research
- validation and limitation statements where model performance or simulation interpretation can be overclaimed

## What Was Excluded

- raw working folders that only make sense to the original researcher
- local environments, caches, and software installs
- incomplete output dumps without interpretation
- overly broad lists of tools without case-study context
- toy examples that would weaken credibility

## Why This Matters

For computational chemistry and pharmaceutical R&D hiring, a portfolio should demonstrate:

- the scientific question
- the data or structure-preparation logic
- the method choice
- the validation strategy
- the interpretive outcome
- the ability to communicate limitations clearly

The EGFR CADD project is presented first because it maps most directly to computational drug-discovery and cheminformatics roles. The materials projects remain important because they show deeper chemical modeling maturity, simulation ownership, and research computing competence.
