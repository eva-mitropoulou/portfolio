Insights based on decomposition behavior
1) Aryl substitution promotes char but lowers onset.
   - Highest char yields are aryl: melamine_aryl (22.0%) and p_phenyldiamine_aryl (39.4%).
   - These show lower T5/T10 relative to ethyl variants despite very high T50.
   - Aryl P-O(ester) bonds are longer and WBI lower vs ethyl, consistent with earlier onset but stronger condensed-phase stabilization (char).

2) Ethyl substitution raises onset but yields almost no char.
   - melamine_ethyl and p_phenyldiamine_ethyl have the highest T5/T10 and single dominant DTG peaks.
   - They also show the shortest P-O(ester) bonds and highest P-O(ester) WBI, consistent with greater bond strength and more volatile decomposition pathways, leading to low residue.

3) Methyl is intermediate for onset but still low char.
   - melamine_methyl T5/T10 sits between aryl and ethyl; char remains low (1.3%).
   - P-O(ester) bond length and WBI are closer to ethyl than aryl, consistent with volatilization-dominated decomposition.

4) Multi-step decomposition is most pronounced for aryl PPD.
   - p_phenyldiamine_aryl shows three DTG peaks (multi-stage breakdown/condensation), while ethyl/methyl show single dominant events.

5) Heuristic relationships across the 5 TGA samples (small n, interpret cautiously):
   - Higher P-O(ester) WBI and shorter P-O(ester) length track with higher T5/T10 and lower char.
   - Higher P=O WBI and longer P-O(ester) length track with higher char.

Prediction for p_phenyldiamine_ch3 (no TGA data)
- Its P-O(ester) length and WBI are close to the ethyl analog.
- Expect onset between aryl and ethyl, but char closer to ethyl (low).



Mechanistic interpretation (from TGA trends + DFT metrics)

Three-step decomposition in aryl systems (why 3 DTG peaks)
- Step 1 (low-mid T): cleavage of weaker peripheral groups (initial P–O(ester) scission and loss of small fragments).
- Step 2 (mid T): further P–O/P–C scission plus onset of condensed-phase cross-linking.
- Step 3 (higher T): slow breakdown of an aromatic/phosphorus-rich char network.

Why aryls yield more char
- Aromatic groups favor condensation and carbonization (ring stabilization and cross-linking), keeping mass in the solid phase.
- Phosphorus functionalities can form P–O–P/polyphosphate-like structures, promoting condensed-phase stabilization.
- Net effect: higher char, higher T50, and multi-step decomposition.

Why alkyls show one dominant step and low char
- Alkyl groups fragment into volatile products (olefins, small amines, alcohols), so little mass remains to form char.
- Stronger P–O(ester) metrics (shorter P–O bonds and higher P–O WBI) concentrate decomposition into a narrower temperature window (single dominant DTG peak).
- Limited aromatic stabilization suppresses long-lived condensed-phase networks.

Likely reactions (generic FR phosphate behavior)
- P–O(ester) cleavage → loss of alkoxy/aryloxy fragments.
- P–C cleavage → detachment of linker groups.
- Condensed-phase phosphorylation → P–O–P network formation.
- Aromatization/cyclization (aryl) → stable carbonaceous char.
- Volatilization (alkyl) → gaseous products, low residue.

How DFT added value in this analysis
- DFT provided quantitative bond-strength surrogates (WBI) and bond lengths that aligned with TGA trends:
  - Shorter P–O(ester) + higher P–O WBI correlated with higher onset (T5/T10) but low char.
  - Longer P–O(ester) + lower P–O WBI correlated with higher char and multi-step behavior.
- NPA/WBI helped rank which bonds are most likely to break first (P–O vs P–C vs N–C), guiding a mechanistic explanation for the DTG peaks.
- The computational data enabled predictions for molecules without TGA (e.g., p_phenyldiamine_ch3) by analogy to structural metrics.
